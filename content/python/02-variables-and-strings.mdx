Last lesson the computer forgot everything the moment it printed it. Today we give it a memory.

<LazyPyodideRepl initialCode={`name = "{{name}}"\nage = {{age}}\nprint(name)\nprint(age)`} />

`name = "{{name}}"` is a **variable**. Read it right to left: take `"{{name}}"`, and store it under the label `name`. Now anywhere below, `name` *is* `"{{name}}"` — you don't have to retype it.

Notice `age = {{age}}` has no quotes. That's because `{{age}}` is a **number** (an `int`), not text. Quotes mean "this is a string, treat it as words, not math." Mix them up and Python will let you know.

You can glue strings together with `+`, or build a sentence with an f-string:

<LazyPyodideRepl initialCode={`name = "{{sibling}}"\nage = {{siblingAge}}\nprint(f"{name} is {age} years old.")`} />

That `f"..."` is an **f-string** — the `f` before the quotes means "look inside the curly braces and fill in the variables." It's the cleanest way to mix text and values, and you'll use it constantly.

Variables can change. That's the whole point of the word "variable":

<LazyPyodideRepl initialCode={`score = 0\nprint(score)\nscore = score + 10\nprint(score)`} />
